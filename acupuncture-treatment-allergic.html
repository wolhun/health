Understanding Allergic Rhinitis

Definition and Symptoms

Allergic rhinitis, commonly known as hay fever, occurs when there’s an allergic reaction to airborne substances. The primary symptoms include sneezing, runny or stuffy nose, itchy eyes, and fatigue. Have you ever felt like your nose was clogged with no relief in sight? That’s the frustration many experience with this condition.

These symptoms can vary in intensity. Some may experience mild irritation, while others suffer from severe discomfort. The condition often flares up in certain seasons, particularly spring and fall, depending on the allergens prevalent at that time. Recognizing these symptoms can be the first step toward relief.

Causes and Triggers

Many factors can contribute to allergic rhinitis. The most common triggers include pollen, dust mites, mold spores, and pet dander. Have you ever wondered why you sneeze more in the spring? That’s likely due to increased pollen in the air.

Environmental factors also play a key role. For example, urban areas often have higher levels of pollutants, which can exacerbate symptoms. Identifying these triggers can help you manage your exposure and potentially alleviate the effects of allergic rhinitis.

Conventional Treatments

Conventional treatments often involve antihistamines and nasal corticosteroids. These medications can relieve symptoms but may not address the underlying causes. Are you tired of taking pills every day? Many people seek alternative approaches for better long-term relief.

Allergy shots, or immunotherapy, are another common option. They involve regular injections to gradually build immunity to allergens. However, this method requires a significant time commitment and may not be suitable for everyone. Finding effective solutions is essential for managing this condition.

Acupoint LI20 (Large Intestine 20)

LI20 is located on either side of the nostrils, about 1 centimeter from the midline of the face. You can find it easily by placing your fingers just below the ridge of your nose. Have you ever tried locating acupuncture points yourself?
